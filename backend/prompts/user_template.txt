EXAMPLE RISKY CLAUSES TO WATCH FOR:                                                                                                                                                                                                                      
                                                            
  1. Early Termination Penalty (HIGH RISK):                                                                                                                                                                                                                
     Original: "Tenant shall be liable for all remaining rent through the end of the lease term plus a fee equal to 2 months rent."                                                                                                                        
     Risk: Double penalty — tenant pays both remaining rent AND a fee. Most states only allow one or the other.                                                                                                                                            
     Recommendation: Negotiate to remove the additional fee, keeping only obligation to pay until unit is re-rented.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           
  2. Automatic Renewal Trap (HIGH RISK):                    
     Original: "This lease shall automatically renew for successive one-year terms unless Tenant provides written notice of non-renewal at least 60 days prior to expiration."                                                                             
     Risk: 60 days is unusually long. Missing this deadline locks tenant into another full year.                                                                                                                                                           
     Recommendation: Request reduction to 30 days notice, which is standard in most states.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           
  3. Landlord Entry Without Notice (CRITICAL):              
     Original: "Landlord may enter the premises at any time for inspection, repairs, or any other purpose."
     Risk: Most states require 24-48 hours advance notice except in emergencies. This clause may be unenforceable.                                                                                                                                         
     Recommendation: Request amendment to require 24-hour written notice except in genuine emergencies.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           
  4. Security Deposit Deduction Loophole (HIGH RISK):       
     Original: "Landlord may deduct from security deposit any amounts deemed necessary for cleaning, repairs, or restoration to original condition."                                                                                                       
     Risk: Vague "deemed necessary" language gives landlord unchecked discretion. No objective standard.
     Recommendation: Add "normal wear and tear excepted" and require itemized deductions with receipts within 21 days.                                                                                                                                     
   
  5. Liability Waiver (HIGH RISK):                                                                                                                                                                                                                         
     Original: "Tenant waives all claims against Landlord for any injury, damage, or loss occurring on the premises."
     Risk: Overbroad liability waivers are often unenforceable and may violate state law, but attempting to enforce them is costly.                                                                                                                        
     Recommendation: Request removal entirely. Landlords cannot legally waive liability for their own negligence in most states.
